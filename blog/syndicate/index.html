The Cleary Cryptocurrency Syndicate

This is the home page for the Cleary Cryptocurrency Syndicate. This syndicate is by invitation only.

Current Status

Total syndicate investment

Core Concept

The main bet of this syndicate is that Ether (Ethereum) will bypass Bitcoin as the cryptocurrency of choice over the next 10 years.

For details, see the "Detailed Expectations" later on this page.

Why a Syndicate?

A syndicate enables better investments. In particular, this syndicate is attempting to have sufficient Ether to "stake" the investment. According to the most current estimates, the syndicate would need to have more than $12,000 in total investments in order to "stake".

Also, I sincerely believe this is a good investment, and a syndicate allows others to join me. This is the third time where my technical knowledge has enabled me to identify a good investment, but it is the first one I am actually taking advantage of.

Mental Model

This is a very risky investment. A mutual fund investment tends to retain some of its value even as it loses value. Consider the stock market as a whole in 1929: Black Monday and Tuesday were losses of only 13% and 12%, and it took 18 months of the Great Depression to lose 89%. In contrast, it is possible for the syndicate investment to actually lose 100%.

Since you can lose the entire investment, this syndicate is more like a crowdfunding than a traditional mutual fund.

You SHOULD NOT INVEST unless you are prepared to sustain a total loss of the money you have invested.

Detailed Expectations and Reasons for Choosing Ethereum

  1. Bitcoin is unmaintainable for global financial solutions. Its proof-of-work system is already costing the global economy more than $1B annually just in electricity and hardware costs. It is my opinion that Bitcoin is now being sustained by hype rather than economics.
  2. Ethereum is a general-purpose blockchain. They have a built-in notion of Smart Contracts, which permits writing programs that are executed by the blockchain itself. While they do have a currency (Ether), the Ethereum blockchain was specifically designed for wider application. In particular, Microsoft has been working with banking systems, developing financial systems using Ethereum technology. (Note that this is Ethereum technology on private block chains and its effect on Ether price is expected to be long-term and indirect; to my knowledge, no bank is designing solutions directly targeting the public Ethereum blockchain).
  3. Ethereum is still fairly new. Although it is past the point of a massively-beneficial early investment, it is still early enough to make a good investment.
  4. Ethereum will be the first cryptocurrency to switch from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake, making it the first sustainable cryptocurrency.
  5. It is possible that Ether will grow to become a "currency of the financial cloud" - a way to execute Ethereum financial applications. This is the primary reason that the syndicate is planning a long-term investment.

Based on evaluations of both Bitcoin and Ethereum markets over their lifetime, in addition to my personal estimate of the overvaluation of Bitcoin, I expect the syndicate investment to return about 20x over the next 10 years. It will probably be less, and could be more, but I would be very surprised if it returned 100x.

Of course, my personal expectation is so speculative that it is essentially meaningless. I am using it as a guideline that will only happen if the syndicate succeeds. I'm including it here just to illustrate that if you invest $1000, don't think that'll set you up for retirement, even if the syndicate is wildly successful.

Risks

The percentages are possibilities that a risk may actually happen. These are all wild guesses.

  1. Bitcoin may remain the primary cryptocurrency, causing Ethereum to fade away (50%). This would most likely happen if Bitcoin itself switches away from a proof-of-work model a short time after Ethereum switches away from the proof-of-work model.
    • Mitigation: None. This syndicate is betting on Ethereum and is not planning to diversify with Bitcoin. The syndicate is betting that the window of "short time" will be too small for Bitcoin to survive. Too soon, and proof-of-stake hasn't been sufficiently proven out by Ether; too late, and Ether has already surpassed Bitcoin.
  2. Ethereum may choose not to switch to proof-of-stake (5%). This would cause some other cryptocurrency to become the primary cryptocurrency, causing Ethereum to fade away.
    • Mitigation: Probably none. The syndicate may choose to diversify to another currency that is going to adopt proof-of-stake, but this is highly unlikely.
  3. Proof-of-stake may be determined to be technically infeasable (10%). This would cause all cryptocurrencies to eventually fail.
    • Mitigation: The syndicate would be dissolved in this situation (at a time chosen by the manager), hopefully cutting losses before all the cryptocurrencies fail.
  4. The first proof-of-stake algorithm may have a major technical flaw, causing Ether to suddenly fail (5%).
    • Mitigation: None. This would be a sudden, catastrophic failure. It would not be possible to detect or avoid it in time.
  5. The proof-of-stake consensus algorithm may not have appropriate default settings in its initial revision, causing the syndicate to lose any "staked" investment (5%).
    • Mitigation: The syndicate is only planning to "stake" portions of its investment (if possible).
  6. Cryptocurrency exchanges may be compromised in a way that criminals are able to steal Ether (95%). Note that this will almost certainly happen over the next decade.
    • Mitigation: The syndicate will hold all its private keys in secure, redundant storage. No part of its long-term investment will be held by any exchange, so none of its Ether would be lost; however, this may cause the price of Ether on the open market to drop.

Structure

The syndicate is comprised of one manager (Stephen Cleary) and multiple investors. The manager is also an investor.

The manager will make most of the decisions for the syndicate; in particular, he will make all the investment decisions and also decide when to dissolve the syndicate.

Initial syndicate investors place their money into a pool, where it is held until the syndicate manager decides it is time for the syndicate to invest (no later than November 10th, 2017).

The initial syndicate investment(s) will have their exchange and transaction fees paid for by the manager. As such, the syndicate starts out in debt to the manager.

As long as the manager is a Microsoft MVP and the syndicate desires to "stake" its investment, the manager commits to providing an Ethereum node for use by the syndicate for staking. This will be the form of a cloud-based machine costing between $50-$125 per month; the value of the Ethereum node is not added to the syndicate's debt; it is a gift of the manager to the syndicate.

New Ether obtained from staking the investment (if any) will be added to the manager's investment. These will be valued at the current Ether price, and used to pay down the debt to the manager.

New investors may join the syndicate at any time. However, new investors will have to pay their own exchange and transaction fees and will enter the Ether market at its current value at that time. Assuming the price of Ether goes up, this means there is a financial benefit to being an initial investor.

Generally, investors may not leave the syndicate until it is dissolved or until 10 years have passed (whichever is first). However, special circumstances will be taken under consideration by the manager.

The manager may periodically put a decision up for a vote to the syndicate. Each investor will have votes equal to the number of Ether they hold within the investment. Unless otherwise specified, the manager will abide by the majority vote (not plurality vote).

When the syndicate is dissolved, it first settles any remaining debt with the manager. Then, each investor will have an option to either transfer their pro rata portion to a wallet under their control, or have the manager cash out their investment. In either case, the investor is responsible for paying the exchange and/or transaction fees.
